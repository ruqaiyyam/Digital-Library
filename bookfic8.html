*To Kill a Mockingbird* is a novel by Harper Lee published in 1960. It takes place in the 1930s in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama, and explores the themes of racial injustice, moral growth, and compassion. The story follows Scout Finch, a young girl, and her father, Atticus Finch, who is defending a black man, Tom Robinson, falsely accused of raping a white woman. Through Scout's eyes, the novel delves into the prejudices and injustices that permeate their society. It has become a classic of modern American literature.
