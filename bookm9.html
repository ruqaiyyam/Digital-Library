"Gone Girl" by Gillian Flynn is a psychological thriller about the complicated and disturbing marriage of Nick and Amy Dunne. When Amy goes missing on their fifth wedding anniversary, Nick becomes the primary suspect in her disappearance. The novel alternates between Nick’s perspective and Amy’s diary entries, unraveling the mystery of what really happened. As the story progresses, it reveals deep secrets, twisted lies, and unexpected plot twists, exposing the manipulations and dark sides of the characters. Themes of deceit, marriage, and the media's role in shaping perceptions are central to the gripping narrative.
