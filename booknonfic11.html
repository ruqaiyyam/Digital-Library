"The Psychology of Money" by Morgan Housel explores the psychological aspects of money and personal finance. The book delves into the behaviors and emotions that influence financial decisions, often more than logical or rational factors. It emphasizes the importance of understanding our own biases, the role of luck, and the power of long-term thinking. Through 20 short stories, Housel explains key principles of money management, illustrating how our relationship with wealth is shaped by emotions, experiences, and social influences. The book provides valuable insights for making better financial decisions and understanding the impact of psychology on wealth.
