"The Time Machine" by H.G. Wells is one of the earliest works of science fiction that explores the concept of time travel. The novel follows an unnamed Time Traveler who invents a machine that allows him to travel through time. After traveling far into the future, he encounters two distinct civilizations: the Eloi, a peaceful but passive race, and the Morlocks, subterranean creatures that prey on the Eloi. Through his adventures, the Time Traveler explores the consequences of human evolution and the potential future of society. The novel raises questions about class struggle, the nature of time, and humanity's future.
