First published in 1813, Pride and Prejudice is a novel by Jane Austen that explores the nature of love, marriage, and society. The story follows the intelligent and strong-willed Elizabeth Bennet, who is one of five daughters in the Bennet family. When Elizabeth meets the wealthy and aloof Mr. Darcy, she is initially repelled by him, but as the story unfolds, she comes to understand that Darcy's pride and her own prejudices have been the obstacles to true love. The novel deals with the themes of social class, courtship, and the pressures of marriage, and is widely considered one of the most important works in English literature.
