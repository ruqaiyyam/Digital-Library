"Letters from a Father to His Daughter" is a collection of letters written by Jawaharlal Nehru, India's first Prime Minister, to his young daughter, Indira Gandhi. The letters, written while Nehru was imprisoned in 1928 and 1929, offer a glimpse into his thoughts on history, science, culture, and the world. Through these letters, Nehru sought to impart knowledge, wisdom, and a sense of curiosity to his daughter. The book is a valuable resource for understanding Nehru's views on various subjects, as well as his deep affection for his daughter and his educational approach.
