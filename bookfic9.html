All the Light We Cannot See by Anthony Doerr is a historical novel set during World War II, following the lives of two characters: Marie-Laure, a blind French girl, and Werner, a German soldier. As the war ravages Europe, their stories unfold in parallel, with Marie-Laure fleeing Paris and Werner being recruited by the Nazis. Their lives eventually intersect in the occupied town of Saint-Malo. The novel explores themes of survival, loss, and the human spirit amidst the devastation of war. It won the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction in 2015.
