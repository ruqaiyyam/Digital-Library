Rebecca by Daphne du Maurier is a haunting tale of love, jealousy, and mystery. The story is narrated by a young, unnamed woman who becomes the second wife of Maxim de Winter. She arrives at Manderley, a grand estate on the English coast, only to find herself overshadowed by the lingering presence of Maxim's first wife, Rebecca. As secrets unravel, the narrator discovers the truth about Rebecca's life and her tragic death. A masterpiece of suspense, *Rebecca* explores themes of identity, power, and the influence of the past on the present.
