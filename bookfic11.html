The Nightingale by Kristin Hannah is a powerful and emotional story set during World War II in Nazi-occupied France. The novel follows the lives of two sisters, Vianne and Isabelle, who have very different ways of coping with the war. Vianne struggles to protect her child and survive under German occupation, while Isabelle joins the French Resistance and risks her life to help downed Allied pilots escape from occupied France. The book explores themes of love, sacrifice, survival, and the resilience of the human spirit in the face of unimaginable hardship.
