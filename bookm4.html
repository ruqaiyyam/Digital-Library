"The Woman in White" by Wilkie Collins is one of the first mystery novels, blending gothic horror and psychological suspense. The story follows Walter Hartright, a drawing teacher who encounters a mysterious woman dressed in white one night, setting off a chain of events that uncovers dark secrets, hidden identities, and wrongful imprisonment. As he becomes entangled with the lives of his students and their families, he uncovers a sinister plot involving a rich inheritance, deception, and a woman who is presumed dead. The novel is known for its compelling structure and dramatic twists, making it a classic in the mystery genre.
