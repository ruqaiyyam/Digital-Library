"Rich Dad Poor Dad" is a personal finance book that contrasts the differing financial mindsets and practices of two father figures in the author's life: his biological father ("Poor Dad") and his best friend's father ("Rich Dad"). Through their stories, Kiyosaki illustrates the importance of financial education, asset building, and understanding the difference between assets and liabilities. The book emphasizes that wealth is achieved not through traditional employment and saving, but through investing in assets that generate income, such as real estate and businesses. Kiyosaki advocates for financial independence by acquiring assets, investing wisely, and understanding how money works. He introduces concepts like the cash flow quadrant, which categorizes individuals as employees, self-employed, business owners, or investors, highlighting the advantages and disadvantages of each quadrant. The overarching message encourages readers to develop a mindset that seeks financial opportunities and to educate themselves about money management to achieve financial freedom.
