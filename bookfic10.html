A Man Called Ove by Fredrik Backman tells the story of Ove, a grumpy, isolated, and curmudgeonly old man who has given up on life after the loss of his wife. His life is turned upside down when a new family moves in next door, and their curious young daughter forms an unlikely friendship with Ove. This touching, humorous novel explores themes of love, loss, and community, and Ove's character evolves from a man hardened by grief to one who rediscovered the value of life and relationships.
