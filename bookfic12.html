The Goldfinch by Donna Tartt tells the story of Theo Decker, a young boy who survives a terrorist attack at an art museum in New York City. In the aftermath, Theo steals a priceless painting, The Goldfinch, setting off a series of events that alter the course of his life. As he grows up, Theo struggles with grief, guilt, and his obsession with the painting, which serves as both a source of comfort and torment. The novel explores themes of loss, survival, and the search for meaning in a chaotic world.
